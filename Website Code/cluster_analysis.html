Cluster Analysis

Cluster Analysis using ArcGIS Map

Insights from Analysis

The above is an ArcGIS interactive web map application that includes a cluster analysis where criminal incidents have occurred in Washington, D.C. from January 1, 2018 to October 31, 2019. Anyone can interact with the map by zooming into particular neighborhoods of the city, then use the bottom right time scale to see how crime has evolved in the areas. The neighborhoods are also clickable which include some brief demographic and population breakdowns to paint a picture what people may be living in the area as victims or possible perpetrators.

At a high level view of the city from the center it would appear that throughout most of 2018 theft while using a vehicle such as an electric scooter or car (blue clusters) were on the rise, but as 2019 progressed most of the vehicle thefts decreased, and theft without a vehicle such as cunningly stealing phones, wallets, etc increased (the red clusters).

If you zoom into the campus of Georgetown University, we can see that theft has also been an issue (primarily red and blue clusters) throughout all of 2018 and in 2019 as well.
